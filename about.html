About ISSTA

We Are ISSTA

ISSTA empowers grassroots communities across Africa to achieve sustainable development by establishing and legitimizing competitive agribusiness cooperatives. By connecting these communities to domestic and international markets, ISSTA drives inclusive economic growth. Additionally, the organization fosters civic participation to promote democracy, human rights, quality service delivery, and enduring peace."

Background

ISSTA has a mission to make contributions to social development and as such, promotes inclusive growth through facilitating small holder farmers (SHFs) to mobilize themselves into feasible grassroots socioeconomic structures, mainly Cooperatives and Village Savings and Loans Associations (VSLAs), under specific value chains, producing and marketing food and tradable products like grain, cocoa, coffee, fish, fruits, and natural ingredients for cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. This approach has proven useful for poverty eradication, Food and Nutrition Security (FNS), and transforming SHFs’ mindsets from subsistence to focused commercial activity. ISSTA also works to improve the target group’s operating environment by building civic engagement capacities, which impact service delivery, sustainable environment resource use, democratic practice, human rights, peace, and justice.

Purpose

ISSTA’s Purpose is to localise sustainable development in all its facets, through the identification of socioeconomic problems and stakeholders’ participatory design and implementation of interventions that ensure inclusive growth amongst grassroots communities in Uganda, and across Africa

ISSTA Key Objective

To catalyse social development with inclusive growth by mobilising smallholder farmers, cooperatives, and MSMEs, to engage in market-focused value chains and promote agri-business, food and nutrition security, sustainable environmental use, and civic engagement

ISSTA Specific Objectives

(1) Inclusive Agribusiness Development: Mobilise smallholder farmers (SHFs)—with a strong focus on women, youth, and marginalized groups—into competitive cooperatives that engage in financially viable, participatory value chains.

(2) Sustainability and Food Security: Mainstream food and nutrition security by promoting sustainable and conservation agriculture practices that ensure the prudent use of environmental resources.

(3) Civic Empowerment and Advocacy: Build community-based capacities to constructively engage duty-bearers, advocating for improved service delivery, human rights, justice, and lasting peace. *Our Stakeholders & Target Groups_ ISSTA bridges the gap between grassroots communities and global development actors. *Our collaborative network includes: Local Communities : Grassroots groups, smallholder farmers, and Community-Based Organisations (CBOs). Governance & Policy Partners: Central governments, local governments, and regional development blocks. Civil Society & Knowledge Partners: National and international NGOs, faith-based organisations, and academia. Global Supporters: International donors and funding partners from across the world.

Programmes

ISSTA Strategic Interventions and impact framework

(i) Cooperative Development:

Mobilise smallholder farmers, fishing communities, artisans, and traders into inclusive, productive, and market-competitive business structures.

(ii) Social Entrepreneurship: _ Promote self-sustaining social enterprises to drive community development and secure long-term institutional sustainability.

(iii) Inclusive Economic Growth: Mainstream women, youth, and marginalised groups into viable, gainful economic activities to bridge social gaps.

(iv) Civic Engagement & Accountability: Empower citizens to engage duty-bearers, demanding transparency, better public services, democracy, human rights, and justice.

(v) Development Advisory Services: Provide Civil Society Organisations (CSOs) and partners with expert support in baseline studies, social research, and project evaluations.

(B) Activities & Key Outputs (What We Do & Produce)

(i) Socio-Economic Structuring: Establishing formal grassroots cooperatives and clusters.

(ii) Capacity Building: Training target groups in competitive business skills and agricultural productivity.

(iii) Civic Action Mobilisation: Creating platforms for citizens to interface with local and national duty-bearers.

(iv) _Research and Insights: Delivering high-quality research, evaluations, and data for development actors.

(C) Intended Outcomes (The Short-to-Medium Term Changes)

(i) Enhanced Market Competitiveness: Grassroots producers access domestic and global value chains directly.

(ii) Responsive Service Delivery: Improved community access to quality education, health, water, sanitation, and environmental management.

(iii) Inclusive Participation: - Active involvement of women and youth in community leadership and economic sectors.

(iv) Strengthened Civil Society: Development partners utilize data-driven insights to design better-targeted interventions.

(D) Long-Term Impact Areas (Our Ultimate Goal)

(i) Poverty Eradication: Sustainable wealth creation and economic independence at the grassroots level.

(ii) Food & Nutrition Security: Climate-resilient, sustainable agricultural systems ensuring year-round food access. (iii) Democratic Governance & Peace: A just society anchored in human rights, institutional accountability, and enduring peace.
